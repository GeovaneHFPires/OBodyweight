==================================================================
DESCRIPTION
==================================================================
[size=6][b]OBodyNG Weight[/b][/size]

[i]Every NPC gets their own body - automatically.[/i]

In most setups, the people of Skyrim tend to share the same handful of figures. OBodyNG Weight changes that. It quietly gives every single NPC their own body as they appear, so a city actually feels like a city: tall and short, slim and curvy, athletic and soft, lean and heavy. You don't collect, sort, or assign anything - install it and the world simply fills with believable, varied people.

Everyone keeps the same look each time you meet them, their clothes fit their body, and it all happens in the background. No menus to babysit, no setup per NPC.

[b]Who it's for[/b]
[list]
[*]You want real body variety without spending hours collecting and assigning BodySlide presets.
[*]You feel everyone in your game looks too similar - or like the same bombshell.
[*]You want a natural crowd: mostly ordinary people, with the occasional striking figure - not a town of clones, and not nothing but pin-ups.
[*]You want bodies AND physics that match, for women and men.
[/list]

[b]What it does, in plain terms[/b]
As each person loads in, the mod builds a body that suits them and remembers it. Most people come out looking natural and average; a smaller number are clearly slim, curvy, athletic or heavy; and a rare few are genuinely striking. Even two people of the same body type now look noticeably different from each other - no more copy-paste crowds. How varied, how curvy, and how common the "striking" bodies are is all up to you in the menu.

[b]Three ways to build bodies[/b] (pick one in the in-game menu)
[list]
[*][b]Procedural[/b] - recommended. Every body is generated from scratch, so you need no presets at all and get the widest variety. Pick this if you don't curate a preset library.
[*][b]OBody Sim Weight[/b] - keeps OBody's preset SHAPE, but re-applies each AUTO-distributed preset at that NPC's own simulated body weight. Two NPCs sharing a preset now differ in size and fullness instead of being identical clones, and presets with no weight range get an automatic lean-to-full range. Presets you assign yourself through OBody's menu are left untouched, so OBody's own features keep working. Pick this to keep your curated preset look while killing the clone army. (Set Weight mode to "NPC Default" for plain OBody with no simulated weight.)
[*][b]Procedural Oriented[/b] - starts from a generated body, then blends it part-way toward that NPC's assigned OBody preset. A slider sets how far: 0% = pure generated, 100% = the preset itself, in between keeps procedural variety and per-NPC size while leaning toward your presets' style. Pick this if you want your presets' flavour without losing variety.
[/list]


==================================================================
HOW TO USE
==================================================================
[list=1]
[*]Install it and load it after OBody NG. Pick a starting flavor in the installer (Realistic, Balanced, or Fantasy - you can change it later).
[*]In game, open the [b]OBodyNG Weight[/b] menu (the mod menu / MCM) and choose a body mode. [b]Procedural[/b] is the easy default.
[*]That's it. Walk around - NPCs get their bodies on their own.
[/list]

[b]Handy extras (all optional)[/b]
[list]
[*][b]Re-roll a body on the spot[/b] - aim at someone and press the re-roll key for a brand-new body for that person. Re-bindable; works in VR (it uses where you're looking).
[*][b]Tune the look[/b] - simple sliders set how varied and how curvy bodies are, how common athletic or unusual figures are, and an overall heavier/leaner dial.
[*][b]Women and men[/b] - turn each on or off independently, and make men slimmer or beefier to taste.
[*][b]Leave certain mods alone[/b] - an Exclusions list shows every mod that adds NPCs, each with a checkbox. Tick a follower or custom-NPC mod and OBW won't touch its hand-made bodies. Your choices are remembered across all your saves.
[*][b]Refresh everyone[/b] - one button re-applies bodies (and physics) to everyone around you after you change a setting, with no waiting or reloading.
[*][b]Fresh start, kept tidy[/b] - want new faces to look different? The menu can hand new bodies to people you haven't met yet, while everyone you've already seen keeps exactly the body they had.
[/list]


==================================================================
MAIN FEATURES
==================================================================
[list]
[*][b]No preset library needed[/b] - you don't download, sort, or assign body presets. Bodies are made for you, for women and men.
[*][b]A whole world of shapes[/b] - a wide range of recognizable body types (slim, pear, hourglass, athletic, curvy, petite, heavy, and many more), each built so the proportions fit together - not random parts thrown on.
[*][b]No two people the same[/b] - even within the same body type, people look clearly different from one another.
[*][b]Natural by default, striking by exception[/b] - most folk look believable and average; the eye-catching bodies are the minority, the way a real crowd looks. Fully adjustable.
[*][b]Physics that fit the body[/b] - with CBPC installed, soft and heavy bodies move more, toned and lean ones stay firm. Optional - without CBPC nothing changes.
[*][b]Clothes follow the body[/b] - armor and outfits fit the shape, with no neck seams or body-vs-clothes mismatch.
[*][b]Women and men[/b] - both supported, each can be switched on or off, plus a build dial for men.
[*][b]Re-roll hotkey[/b] - a new body for anyone, instantly. Works in VR.
[*][b]Exclude any NPC mod[/b] - a simple checkbox list keeps OBW off the followers and custom NPCs you'd rather leave as their authors made them.
[*][b]Consistent and tidy[/b] - everyone keeps their look between visits; generating fresh bodies only affects people you haven't met yet.
[*][b]Light and unobtrusive[/b] - easy on performance, takes no load-order slot. Works on Skyrim SE, AE and VR.
[/list]


==================================================================
INSTALLATION
==================================================================
[list=1]
[*]Install with a mod manager (MO2 / Vortex) and enable [b]OBodyNGWeight.esp[/b], loaded after OBody NG.
[*]Pick a starting flavor in the installer - Realistic, Balanced, or Fantasy (changeable anytime in the menu).
[*]In game, open the "OBodyNG Weight" menu and choose the [b]Procedural[/b] body mode.
[*]Recommended: in OBody's own menu, set its NPC-selection hotkey to [b]None[/b], so the re-roll key only triggers this mod.
[/list]
Make sure your CBBE / 3BA (female) and HIMBO (male) bodies are built in BodySlide with [b]"Build Morphs" checked[/b]. Build your armors with morphs too if you want clothing to follow the body.

==================================================================
FAQ
==================================================================
[b]Do I need to collect BodySlide presets?[/b]
No. In Procedural mode the mod builds every body itself. If you already love a preset collection, the other modes use it - just with per-NPC variety.

[b]Will my followers / custom NPCs change?[/b]
Only if you let them. Add their mod to the Exclusions list (a checkbox) and OBW leaves them exactly as their author made them.

[b]Does it change an NPC's face or weight number?[/b]
No. It only shapes the body, and it keeps faces and outfits matching - no neck seams.

[b]Will the same NPC look different every time I see them?[/b]
No - everyone keeps their body. You can deliberately re-roll one person with a hotkey, or hand fresh bodies to people you haven't met yet, but nobody changes behind your back.

[b]Men too?[/b]
Yes. Women and men are both supported and can be toggled independently.
